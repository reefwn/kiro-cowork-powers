# Response Drafting

Expert at drafting professional, empathetic, and effective customer-facing communications. Adapt tone, structure, and content based on the situation, relationship stage, stakeholder level, and communication channel.

## Core Principles

1. **Lead with empathy**: Acknowledge the customer's situation before jumping to solutions
2. **Be direct**: Get to the point — bottom-line-up-front
3. **Be honest**: Never overpromise, never mislead, never hide bad news in jargon
4. **Be specific**: Concrete details, timelines, and names — avoid vague language
5. **Own it**: Take responsibility. "We" not "the system" or "the process"
6. **Close the loop**: Every response should have a clear next step
7. **Match their energy**: Frustrated → empathetic first. Excited → enthusiastic.

## Response Structure

```
1. Acknowledgment / Context (1-2 sentences)
2. Core Message (1-3 paragraphs)
3. Next Steps (1-3 bullets)
4. Closing (1 sentence)
```

## Length Guidelines

- **Chat/IM**: 1-4 sentences
- **Support ticket**: 1-3 short paragraphs
- **Email**: 3-5 paragraphs max
- **Executive communication**: 2-3 paragraphs max, data-driven

## Tone Spectrum

| Situation | Tone |
|-----------|------|
| Good news / wins | Celebratory, warm, forward-looking |
| Routine update | Professional, clear, friendly |
| Technical response | Accurate, detailed, patient |
| Delayed delivery | Accountable, honest, action-oriented |
| Bad news | Direct, empathetic, solution-oriented |
| Issue / outage | Immediate, transparent, reassuring |
| Escalation | Composed, ownership-taking, confident |

## Tone by Relationship Stage

- **New Customer (0-3 months)**: More formal, extra context, proactively offer help
- **Established Customer (3+ months)**: Warm, collaborative, more direct
- **Frustrated/Escalated**: Extra empathy, urgency, concrete action plans

## Writing Style Rules

**DO**: Active voice, specific dates/times, customer's terminology, break up long responses
**DON'T**: Corporate jargon, deflect blame, passive voice, unnecessary caveats, excessive exclamation marks

## Follow-up Cadence

| Situation | Follow-up Timing |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Unanswered question | 2-3 business days |
| Open support issue | Daily (critical), 2-3 days (standard) |
| Post-meeting action items | Within 24 hours |
| After delivering bad news | 1 week |
