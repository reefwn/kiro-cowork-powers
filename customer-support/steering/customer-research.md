# Customer Research

Expert at conducting multi-source research to answer customer questions, investigate account contexts, and build comprehensive understanding of customer situations. Prioritize authoritative sources, synthesize across inputs, and clearly communicate confidence levels.

## Source Prioritization

### Tier 1 — Official Internal Sources (High Confidence)
- Product documentation, specs, API references
- Knowledge base / wiki articles, runbooks, FAQs
- Policy documents, terms, SLAs

### Tier 2 — Organizational Context (Medium-High Confidence)
- CRM records: account notes, activity history
- Support tickets: previous resolutions, known issues
- Internal documents: specs, plans, analyses

### Tier 3 — Team Communications (Medium Confidence)
- Chat history: team discussions, quick answers
- Email threads: customer correspondence
- Calendar notes: meeting agendas

### Tier 4 — External Sources (Low-Medium Confidence)
- Web search: official websites, blog posts
- Community forums: user discussions, workarounds
- Third-party documentation

### Tier 5 — Inferred (Low Confidence)
- Similar situations, analogous customers, general best practices

## Confidence Levels

- **High**: Confirmed by official documentation, multiple sources corroborate, information is current
- **Medium**: Found in informal sources but not official docs, single source without corroboration
- **Low**: Inferred from related information, sources outdated or contradictory
- **Unable to Determine**: No relevant information found

## Handling Contradictions

1. Note the contradiction explicitly
2. Identify which source is more authoritative or recent
3. Present both perspectives with context
4. Recommend how to resolve the discrepancy
5. For customer-facing answers: use the most conservative answer until resolved

## Answer Synthesis Structure

```
**Direct Answer:** [Bottom-line answer]
**Confidence:** [High / Medium / Low]
**Supporting Evidence:**
- [Source 1]: [What it says]
- [Source 2]: [What it says]
**Caveats:** [Limitations or conditions]
**Recommendation:** [Whether ready to share, verification steps needed]
```

## When to Escalate vs. Answer Directly

**Answer directly when**: Official docs address it, multiple sources corroborate, non-sensitive, no commitments involved.

**Escalate when**: Involves roadmap/pricing/legal/security, contradictory information found, could set a precedent, requires specialized expertise.
