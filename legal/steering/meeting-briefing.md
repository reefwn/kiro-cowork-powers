# Meeting Briefing

Prepare structured briefings for meetings with legal relevance and track resulting action items.

## Meeting Prep Methodology

### 1. Identify the Meeting

Determine from the user's request or calendar:
- Meeting title and type (deal review, board meeting, vendor call, team sync, client meeting, regulatory discussion)
- Participants and their roles
- Agenda or expected topics
- Your role (advisor, presenter, observer, negotiator)
- Preparation time available

### 2. Assess Preparation Needs

| Meeting Type | Key Prep Needs |
|---|---|
| Deal Review | Contract status, open issues, counterparty history, negotiation strategy |
| Board / Committee | Legal updates, risk register highlights, pending matters, regulatory developments |
| Vendor Call | Agreement status, open issues, performance metrics, negotiation objectives |
| Team Sync | Workload status, priority matters, resource needs, upcoming deadlines |
| Client / Customer | Agreement terms, support history, open issues, relationship context |
| Regulatory | Matter background, compliance status, prior communications |
| Litigation / Dispute | Case status, recent developments, strategy, settlement parameters |

### 3. Gather Context from Connected Sources

Pull from each connected source:
- **Calendar**: Meeting details, prior meetings with same participants, related meetings
- **Email**: Recent correspondence, prior follow-up threads, open action items
- **Chat**: Recent discussions, messages from participants, relevant decisions
- **Documents**: Agendas, prior meeting notes, relevant agreements
- **CLM**: Relevant contracts, negotiation status, approval workflows
- **CRM**: Account info, relationship history, deal stage

### 4. Synthesize into Briefing

```
## Meeting Brief

### Meeting Details
- **Meeting**: [title]
- **Date/Time**: [date and time]
- **Duration**: [expected duration]
- **Location**: [physical or video link]
- **Your Role**: [advisor / presenter / negotiator / observer]

### Participants
| Name | Organization | Role | Key Interests | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|

### Agenda / Expected Topics

### Background and Context

### Key Documents

### Open Issues
| Issue | Status | Owner | Priority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|

### Legal Considerations

### Talking Points

### Questions to Raise

### Decisions Needed

### Red Lines / Non-Negotiables

### Prior Meeting Follow-Up

### Preparation Gaps
```

### 5. Identify Preparation Gaps

Flag anything that could not be found or verified: unavailable sources, outdated information, unanswered questions, missing documents.

## Action Item Tracking

After the meeting, help capture action items:

```
## Action Items from [Meeting Name] — [Date]

| # | Action Item | Owner | Deadline | Priority | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
```

Best practices:
- Be specific (not "follow up on contract" but "send redline of Section 4.2")
- Assign exactly one owner per item
- Set a specific deadline date
- Note dependencies
- Distinguish: legal team actions, business team actions, external actions, follow-up meetings

Follow-up: distribute action items, set calendar reminders, update relevant systems, file meeting notes, flag urgent items.
