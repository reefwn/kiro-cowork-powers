# Legal Risk Assessment

Assess and classify legal risks using a severity-by-likelihood framework with escalation criteria.

## Risk Assessment Framework

### Severity x Likelihood Matrix

**Severity** (impact if the risk materializes):

| Level | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Negligible | Minor inconvenience; no material financial, operational, or reputational impact |
| 2 | Low | Limited impact; minor financial exposure (< 1% of contract value) |
| 3 | Moderate | Meaningful impact; material financial exposure (1-5% of value); potential limited public attention |
| 4 | High | Significant impact; substantial financial exposure (5-25% of value); likely public attention; potential regulatory scrutiny |
| 5 | Critical | Severe impact; major financial exposure (> 25% of value); significant reputational damage; regulatory action likely |

**Likelihood** (probability the risk materializes):

| Level | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Remote | Highly unlikely; no known precedent |
| 2 | Unlikely | Could occur but not expected; limited precedent |
| 3 | Possible | May occur; some precedent exists |
| 4 | Likely | Probably will occur; clear precedent |
| 5 | Almost Certain | Expected to occur; triggering events present or imminent |

**Risk Score = Severity x Likelihood**

| Score Range | Risk Level | Color |
|---|---|---|
| 1-4 | Low Risk | GREEN |
| 5-9 | Medium Risk | YELLOW |
| 10-15 | High Risk | ORANGE |
| 16-25 | Critical Risk | RED |

## Risk Classification Levels

### GREEN — Low Risk (1-4)
Accept and document. Monitor in periodic reviews. No escalation required.

### YELLOW — Medium Risk (5-9)
Mitigate with specific controls. Monitor actively (monthly). Assign owner. Brief stakeholders. Define trigger events for elevation.

### ORANGE — High Risk (10-15)
Escalate to senior counsel. Develop mitigation plan. Brief leadership. Consider outside counsel. Document in detail with contingency plan.

### RED — Critical Risk (16-25)
Immediate escalation to General Counsel/C-suite/Board. Engage outside counsel. Establish response team. Consider insurance notification. Preserve evidence. Daily or more frequent review.

## When to Escalate to Outside Counsel

**Mandatory**: Active litigation, government investigation, criminal exposure, securities issues, board-level matters.

**Strongly Recommended**: Novel legal issues, jurisdictional complexity, material financial exposure, specialized expertise needed, regulatory changes, M&A transactions.

**Consider**: Complex contract disputes, employment matters, data incidents, IP disputes, insurance coverage disputes.
