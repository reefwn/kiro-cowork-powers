# Drug Discovery

Search compounds, drug targets, and clinical trials using connected databases.

## Workflow

### 1. Target Identification
- Search ~~drug targets (Open Targets) for disease-associated targets
- Review genetic associations, known drugs, and tractability assessments
- Prioritize targets by evidence strength and druggability

### 2. Compound Search
- Search ~~chemical database (ChEMBL) for bioactive compounds against targets
- Filter by activity type, potency, and selectivity
- Review ADMET properties and drug-likeness

### 3. Clinical Trial Landscape
- Search ~~clinical trials (ClinicalTrials.gov) for ongoing/completed trials
- Identify competitive landscape and unmet needs
- Review trial designs and endpoints for similar indications

### 4. Literature Context
- Search ~~literature for recent publications on targets and compounds
- Access preprints for cutting-edge findings
- Review mechanism of action and safety data

### 5. Synthesis
- Summarize target validation evidence
- Rank compound candidates by potency, selectivity, and drug-likeness
- Map clinical trial landscape and identify gaps
- Recommend next steps (hit-to-lead, lead optimization, etc.)

## Output Format

Provide structured results with:
- Target summary (gene, protein, disease associations, tractability)
- Compound hits (structure, activity, selectivity, ADMET flags)
- Clinical trial overview (phase, status, endpoints, competitors)
- Recommended strategy with rationale
