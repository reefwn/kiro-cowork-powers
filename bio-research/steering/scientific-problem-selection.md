# Scientific Problem Selection

Systematic framework for research problem selection based on Fischbach & Walsh's framework.

## Entry Points

Present users with three options:

1. **Pitch an idea for a new project** — work it up together
2. **Share a problem in a current project** — troubleshoot together
3. **Ask a strategic question** — navigate the decision tree together

## Option 1: Pitch an Idea

Ask: "Tell me the short version of your idea (1-2 sentences)."

Then ask for detail:
1. What exactly you want to do
2. How you currently plan to do it
3. If it works, why will it be a big deal
4. What you think are the major risks

Guide through: Intuition Pumps → Risk Assessment → Optimization Function → Parameter Strategy

## Option 2: Troubleshoot a Problem

Ask: "Tell me a short version of your problem (1-2 sentences)."

Then ask for detail:
1. The overall goal of your project
2. What exactly went wrong
3. Your current ideas for fixing it

Guide through: Decision Tree Navigation → Parameter Strategy → Adversity Response → Problem Inversion

## Option 3: Strategic Question

Ask: "Tell me the short version of your question (1-2 sentences)."

Then ask for detail:
1. The setting (current or future project)
2. More detail about what you're thinking

Draw on appropriate skills based on the question.

## The 9 Skills

| Skill | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| 1. Intuition Pumps | Generate high-quality research ideas |
| 2. Risk Assessment | Identify and manage project risks |
| 3. Optimization Function | Define success metrics |
| 4. Parameter Strategy | Decide what to fix vs. keep flexible |
| 5. Decision Tree Navigation | Plan decision points |
| 6. Adversity Response | Prepare for crises as opportunities |
| 7. Problem Inversion | Navigate around obstacles |
| 8. Integration & Synthesis | Synthesize into coherent plan |
| 9. Meta-Framework | Orchestrate complete workflow |

## Core Principles

- **Problem Choice >> Execution Quality** — even brilliant execution of a mediocre problem yields incremental impact
- **Don't avoid risk — befriend it** — no risk = incremental work, but multiple miracles = avoid
- **Fix ONE meaningful constraint** — too many fixed = brittleness, too few = paralysis
- **Crises are inevitable and opportune** — fix the problem AND upgrade the project simultaneously

## Audience

- **Graduate Students** — thesis projects, qualifying exams (Skills 1-3 + 9)
- **Postdocs** — new positions, fellowship applications (all skills)
- **PIs** — new directions, mentoring, grant cycles (Skills 1, 3, 4, 6)
- **Startup Founders** — inception, pivots, pitches (Skills 1-4 + 8)

## Reference

Fischbach, M.A., & Walsh, C.T. (2024). "Problem choice and decision trees in science and engineering." *Cell*, 187, 1828-1833.
